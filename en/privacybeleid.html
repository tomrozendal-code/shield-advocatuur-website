Privacy policy

In Shield Advocatuur’s privacy statement you can read how we handle your personal data.

Last updated: 22 July 2026.

Shield Advocatuur attaches great importance to the protection of your personal data and processes it carefully, in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the applicable rules of professional conduct for the legal profession, including the duty of confidentiality. This privacy policy explains which personal data we process, for what purpose, and what rights you have.

1. Data controller

The data controller is Shield Advocatuur, with offices at Gustav Mahlerplein 2, 1082 MD Amsterdam and Baarnsche Dijk 4, 3741 LR Baarn, registered with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce under number [CoC number]. You can reach us at contact@shieldadvocatuur.nl or +31 (0)35 7850717.

2. Which personal data we process

Depending on the services provided, we may process the following data:

  • contact and identification data, such as name, address, place of residence, telephone number and e-mail address;
  • data required to handle your matter, which may include special categories of personal data and criminal-law data, insofar as necessary for the legal services;
  • financial and administrative data, such as bank account number and invoicing details;
  • data we are required to establish under the Wwft, such as a copy of an identity document;
  • technical data you leave behind when visiting our website or using the contact form, such as your IP address.

3. Purposes and legal bases

We process your personal data for the following purposes and on the following legal bases:

  • performing the engagement and providing legal services — on the basis of the performance of the contract;
  • complying with legal obligations, including the Wwft and tax retention duties — on the basis of a legal obligation;
  • invoicing and accounts management — on the basis of the performance of the contract and our legitimate interest;
  • handling your enquiry via the contact form or by e-mail — on the basis of our legitimate interest in proper communication;
  • improving and securing our website — on the basis of our legitimate interest.

4. Sharing with third parties and processors

We only share your personal data with third parties where necessary to perform the engagement or to comply with a legal obligation. These may include, for example, courts, opposing parties and their lawyers, bailiffs or engaged experts. We also use service providers that process data on our instructions (processors), such as suppliers of IT, hosting and administration services. We conclude processing agreements with these processors. Your data is not sold or provided to third parties for commercial purposes.

5. Transfers outside the European Economic Area

In principle, your personal data is processed within the European Economic Area (EEA). If data is nevertheless processed outside the EEA, we ensure appropriate safeguards, such as the standard contractual clauses adopted by the European Commission.

6. Retention periods

We do not retain your personal data longer than necessary. In principle, file data is retained for at least seven years after completion of the matter, unless a longer statutory or professional retention period applies. Data we are required to retain under tax or Wwft legislation is kept for the applicable statutory periods.

7. Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against loss or unlawful processing, including access controls, encryption where appropriate, and confidentiality arrangements with our staff and processors.

8. Your rights

You have the right to access, rectify or erase your personal data. You may also ask us to restrict the processing, object to the processing and exercise your right to data portability. Insofar as the processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time. These rights may be limited by our statutory duty of confidentiality and retention obligations. You can submit a request at contact@shieldadvocatuur.nl; to verify your identity we may ask for additional information.

9. Cookies

Our website makes limited use of cookies and similar techniques. You will find more information in our cookie policy.

10. Complaint to the Dutch Data Protection Authority

If you believe that we do not handle your personal data carefully, please contact us. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens), P.O. Box 93374, 2509 AJ The Hague (autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl).

11. Changes

We may amend this privacy policy from time to time. The most recent version is always available on this page. We advise you to consult this policy periodically.
