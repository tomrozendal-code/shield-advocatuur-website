Terms & conditions

Last updated: 22 July 2026.

1. Definitions

In these general terms and conditions, Shield Advocatuur means Shield Advocatuur B.V., established in Amsterdam and Baarn, registered with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce under number 95177299 and with VAT identification number NL867030653B01. Client means the natural or legal person who instructs Shield Advocatuur. Engagement means the contract for services within the meaning of Article 7:400 of the Dutch Civil Code.

2. Applicability

These general terms and conditions apply to every engagement given to Shield Advocatuur, including any follow-up, amended or supplementary engagement, and to all legal relationships arising from or connected with it. Deviations from these terms are only valid if agreed in writing. The applicability of any general terms of the client is expressly rejected.

3. The engagement

All engagements are accepted and performed exclusively by Shield Advocatuur, even where it is the express or implied intention that an engagement is carried out by a particular person. The operation of Articles 7:404, 7:407(2) and 7:409 of the Dutch Civil Code is excluded. Shield Advocatuur will use its best efforts to achieve the intended result but cannot guarantee it; the obligation is one of best efforts.

4. Engaging third parties

Shield Advocatuur is entitled to engage third parties in performing an engagement, such as bailiffs, foreign lawyers, civil-law notaries, accountants or other experts. Such third parties are selected with due care and, where possible, in consultation with the client. Shield Advocatuur is not liable for any shortcomings of these third parties and is authorised to accept, also on behalf of the client, any limitations of liability stipulated by them.

5. Fees, disbursements and office costs

Unless agreed otherwise in writing, fees are calculated on the basis of the hours spent multiplied by the applicable hourly rate, plus disbursements (such as court fees, bailiff and translation costs) and any VAT due by law. Shield Advocatuur is entitled to adjust its rates periodically, and at least annually. Shield Advocatuur may require an advance payment before starting work; an advance is set off against the final invoice.

6. Payment

Invoices must be paid within fourteen days of the invoice date, without suspension, deduction or set-off, into the bank account designated by Shield Advocatuur. If the payment term is exceeded, the client is in default by operation of law and owes statutory interest as well as the reasonable costs of obtaining payment out of court. In that case Shield Advocatuur is entitled to suspend its work, observing the care that may be expected of a lawyer.

7. Liability

Any liability of Shield Advocatuur is limited to the amount paid out in the relevant case under its professional liability insurance, increased by the deductible payable by Shield Advocatuur under that insurance. The professional liability insurance meets at least the requirements set by the Netherlands Bar (Nederlandse Orde van Advocaten). The insurance is placed with [name of insurer] with cover of [cover amount] per claim.

If for any reason no payment is made under the professional liability insurance, any liability is limited to the fees charged by Shield Advocatuur in the relevant matter during the relevant calendar year, up to a maximum of € 25,000. Every claim lapses if it is not submitted to Shield Advocatuur in writing within twelve months after the client became aware of the damage and of the liable party.

8. Third-party funds

Shield Advocatuur does not operate a third-party funds account (derdengeldenrekening) and has no third-party funds foundation. Funds due to the client or to third parties are not held by Shield Advocatuur. Shield Advocatuur is entitled to set off amounts owed to the client against outstanding invoices, insofar as the applicable rules of conduct permit.

9. Identification and the Dutch Money Laundering Act (Wwft)

The Dutch Act on the Prevention of Money Laundering and Financing of Terrorism (Wwft) may apply to the services of Shield Advocatuur. Where it does, Shield Advocatuur is required, among other things, to establish the identity of the client and of any ultimate beneficial owners and, where applicable, to report unusual transactions to the designated authority without notifying the client. By giving the engagement, the client confirms that it is aware of this and will cooperate.

10. Complaints and disputes

Shield Advocatuur strives for a high quality of service. If the client is dissatisfied with the formation or performance of an engagement, with the quality of the service or with the amount of an invoice, the client must first submit its objections in writing to Shield Advocatuur, for the attention of the complaints officer. Shield Advocatuur will seek a solution together with the client within a reasonable period. 11. Retention and destruction of files

After completion of an engagement, the documents in the file are retained for at least seven years, after which Shield Advocatuur is entitled to destroy the file without further notice. Original documents are returned to the client on request.

12. Governing law and competent court

The legal relationship between Shield Advocatuur and the client is governed exclusively by Dutch law. Disputes that are not settled amicably or through the complaints and disputes scheme are submitted to the competent court of the District Court of Amsterdam, without prejudice to the right of Shield Advocatuur to bring a dispute before the court having jurisdiction by law.
