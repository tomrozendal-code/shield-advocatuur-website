Corporate Governance

Corporate Governance & Representation

Shield Advocaten supports companies, shareholders, directors and investors in the legal and practical organisation of their corporate governance. Our role extends beyond advice alone: where desired, we also act on our clients’ behalf and give effect to their decision-making — with the diligence, discretion and authority that corporate practice demands.

Our services include, among other things:

  • organising, preparing and chairing general meetings of shareholders (AGMs) and extraordinary shareholder meetings;
  • drafting agendas, notices, minutes, shareholder resolutions and other corporate documentation;
  • representing shareholders, directors and investors during meetings and negotiations;
  • exercising voting rights and performing legal acts under power of attorney;
  • handling the legal and administrative formalities surrounding corporate decision-making;
  • guiding share transfers, investments, restructurings and other corporate transactions;
  • temporarily fulfilling a managerial or supervisory role, insofar as the law and circumstances permit;
  • acting as an independent representative where interests within a company diverge.

Continuity in shareholder disputes

Within family businesses, joint ventures and other companies, situations regularly arise in which shareholders find themselves at odds. Where two shareholders each hold an equal interest, decision-making can grind to a complete halt — a deadlock that not only strains mutual relations but also affects the continuity of the business.

In such situations, Shield Advocaten is regularly asked to assume an independent role. This may involve acting on behalf of a minority shareholder, exercising shareholder rights under power of attorney, or — where appropriate and legally desirable — taking up a limited, independent shareholding position. The interest of any one of the disputing parties is never paramount; rather, the focus is on careful, legally sound decision-making, with balanced regard for both the interests of the minority shareholder and the continuity and corporate interest of the company.

Our efforts are consistently directed at preventing escalation, safeguarding governance stability and creating room for a lasting solution.

A practical legal partner

Corporate law does not end with the drafting of contracts. In day-to-day practice, there is often a need for an independent lawyer who can both advise and act. Whether it concerns a shareholders’ meeting, a board resolution, a restructuring, an investment or a conflict within the company: we ensure that the legal foundation is in order and that decision-making proceeds carefully, independently and efficiently.
