One generation on: the careful transfer of a family business

The transfer of a family business is where emotion, wealth and entrepreneurship converge. Shield Advocaten guided a family through a handover that left both the business and the family relationships intact.

More than a transaction

The founder of a successful family business wished to hand over the reins gradually to two of his three children — one of whom had worked in the business for years, while the third stood outside it. A familiar situation, with a familiar risk: that the commercial handover would strain the family relationships.

Our approach

We approached the succession not as a single moment, but as a process. In conversations with everyone involved, we mapped interests and expectations and translated them into clear legal arrangements: a phased share transfer, a shareholders’ agreement, a balanced arrangement for the non-active child and safeguards for the board.

A succession succeeds when no one feels passed over and the business does not stand still for a single day.

We coordinated the tax and notarial aspects with the family’s regular advisers, so that the handover was balanced in legal, fiscal and human terms alike.

The outcome

The business is now in the hands of the next generation, control is clearly allocated and the non-active family member has been fairly compensated. The founder remained involved as an adviser — at a distance, but with confidence.
