Command over cross-border wealth: an international family restructures with the next generation in mind

A wealthy family with interests across several jurisdictions faced the question of how to organise its assets in a future-proof, transparent and manageable way. Shield Advocaten guided the entire restructuring.

The starting point

The family — third-generation entrepreneurs — had built its wealth over decades into a web of companies, real estate and holdings, spread across several countries. What had once been efficient had grown into an opaque structure in which control, liability and taxation were no longer aligned.

The objective was clear: a single, transparent governance framework, protection of the family wealth and careful preparation for the transition to the next generation — without jeopardising the continuity of the underlying businesses.

Our approach

Shield Advocaten first mapped the full legal and control structure and identified the pressure points. Working closely with the family’s tax advisers and civil-law notary, we then designed a new holding structure with a family charter as the compass for decision-making, succession and dispute resolution.

Sound governance within a family does not begin with the structure, but with the agreements that underpin it.

Where desired, we acted not only in an advisory capacity but also in execution: on the certification of shares, the establishment of a trust-office foundation and the formalisation of decision-making within the family.

The outcome

The family now has a transparent, robust structure in which wealth and control are separated, risks are ring-fenced and the transfer to the next generation can take place step by step. Above all, there is calm: the rules were settled before the first question arose.
